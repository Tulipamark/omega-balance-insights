# Portal Lifecycle

## Purpose

This document defines who exists in our system, why they exist there, and when
someone should get portal access.

The key rule is simple:

- Zinzino owns the official network, rank, placement, qualification, and payout
  logic
- our platform owns attribution, lead capture, review, onboarding support, and
  the working layer for our own team

This means not everyone in the wider Zinzino structure should become a portal
user in our system.

## Lifecycle States

### 1. Visitor

Definition:

- someone lands on our pages
- reads, clicks, or browses
- may continue into a Zinzino flow or leave

What we store:

- traffic
- attribution
- visit and click signals

Portal access:

- no

Why this matters:

- visitors are part of inflow, not part of our portal team

### 2. Customer Lead

Definition:

- someone shows customer intent
- submits a form, contact request, or other lead signal

What we store:

- lead record
- attribution
- contact details when provided
- follow-up status

Portal access:

- no

Why this matters:

- customer leads are highly valuable for ZZ volume, points, and credits
- but they do not need a portal account with us

### 3. Partner Lead

Definition:

- someone shows initial partner interest through our funnel

What we store:

- lead record
- attribution
- application details
- review status

Portal access:

- no, not by default

Why this matters:

- partner interest is not the same as team membership
- we should not create accounts too early

### 4. Partner Candidate

Definition:

- a partner lead we actively review and consider for our team model
- someone we may contact, qualify, and support toward the next step

What we store:

- review notes
- priority
- candidate status
- follow-up history

Portal access:

- still no, unless there is a clear reason to bring the person into our working
  layer

Why this matters:

- candidates belong in admin review first
- they do not automatically become portal users

### 5. Team Member

Definition:

- someone who has actively joined Zinzino
- someone who wants to build with us, not just sit in the wider structure
- someone who uses our support, guidance, links, and operating model

What we store:

- portal user
- referral code
- partner-facing guidance and progress data
- relationship data needed for our own follow-up layer

Portal access:

- yes

Why this matters:

- team membership starts after real Zinzino join, not at lead stage
- the portal is for our team layer
- not for every person in the wider Zinzino structure

## What This Means In Practice

### Customers matter, but do not need portal accounts

Customers are important because they drive:

- volume
- points
- credits
- momentum in Zinzino

But in our system they usually remain:

- customer leads
- attributed customers
- follow-up contacts

They do not automatically become portal users.

### Partner interest does not equal portal access

Someone can:

- show partner interest
- be reviewed by us
- attend a Zoom call or presentation
- even move forward toward Zinzino

without becoming part of our portal team layer.

Portal access should be intentional, not automatic.

### Team access starts after real commitment

Someone should not become a team member in our portal just because they:

- filled in a partner form
- sounded promising
- attended a call

Team membership should begin when the person:

- has actively joined Zinzino
- and wants to build with us in practice

### Our platform is for our team, not the whole network

Our platform should serve:

- our working team
- our direct support model
- our own operating rhythm

It should not try to mirror:

- the full Zinzino tree
- all ranks
- all placements
- the full network around us

## Product Implications

### Admin should focus on

- visitors and attribution
- customer leads
- partner leads
- partner candidates
- active team members in our portal

### Partner dashboard should be for

- team members in our portal
- not all Zinzino partners in our wider structure

### Data model should stay lighter than Zinzino

We should avoid building:

- a duplicate network tree
- duplicate rank logic
- duplicate official partner administration

We should keep:

- attribution
- leads
- candidate review
- team-member support

## Guardrail

If a new idea requires us to ask:

"Do all people in the wider Zinzino structure need to exist as full users in our portal?"

the default answer should be:

- no

Only the people who are truly part of our team layer should become portal users.
