# Team Engagement Levels

## Purpose

This document defines simple engagement levels inside our own team layer.

It is not a copy of Zinzino rank logic.

It exists to help us decide:

- who is still under review
- who is part of our working team
- who belongs in the tighter core

## Guardrail

This is not:

- a payout model
- a prestige ladder for its own sake
- a duplicate of Zinzino rank or placement

This is:

- an operating model for access, support, and closeness to the core

## Level 1 - Candidate

Definition:

- someone who has shown partner interest
- someone we are following up
- someone who may be invited to calls, presentations, or direct follow-up

Not yet:

- a team member
- a portal member by default
- part of the inner core

Typical signs:

- filled in a partner form
- responded to follow-up
- attended or is invited to a Zoom call

Main need:

- clarity, follow-up, and a path toward a real decision

## Level 2 - Team Member

Definition:

- someone who has actively joined Zinzino
- someone who wants to build with us
- someone who should receive portal access and team support

Typical signs:

- active Zinzino join is complete
- wants to use our working model
- should receive links, guidance, and follow-up

Main need:

- onboarding
- first result
- structure

## Level 3 - Core

Definition:

- a team member who is not only present, but actually building
- someone who shows repeat activity and closer alignment with our model
- someone who belongs in the tighter working circle

Typical signs:

- shows up consistently
- follows through
- creates customer or partner movement
- wants closer support and rhythm with us

Possible support:

- private Facebook group
- tighter Zoom rhythm
- closer follow-up
- more direct working access

Main need:

- momentum
- repetition
- support toward real duplication

## How To Use These Levels

### Candidate

Use for:

- review
- calls
- presentations
- follow-up

### Team Member

Use for:

- portal access
- first-result support
- links and operating guidance

### Core

Use for:

- tighter inner-circle collaboration
- more direct support
- closer working rhythm

## Product Implications

Admin should be able to distinguish between:

- candidate
- team member
- core

But the first product step is simple:

- first separate candidate from team member clearly
- later, add a lightweight way to identify core members

## Practical Rule

No one becomes a team member in our portal before they:

- actively join Zinzino
- and want to build with us

No one should be treated as core just because they:

- sound excited
- attend one call
- say the right things

Core should be based on real engagement, not enthusiasm alone.
